# I Introduction
## Recommended Problems
### P1.1
Evaluate each of the following expressions for the complex number \(z = \frac{1}{2} e^{j \pi / 4}\)
1.  \(\operatorname{Re}\{z\}\)
2.  \(\operatorname{Im}\{z\}\)
3.  \(|z|\)
4.  \(\measuredangle z\)
5.  \(z^{*}\) (\(*\) denotes complex conjugation)
6.  \(z+z^{*}\)

### P1.2
Let \(z\) be an arbitrary complex number.
1.  Show that
    $$\operatorname{Re}(z)=\frac{z+z^{*}}{2}$$
2.  Show that
    $$j \operatorname{Im}(z)=\frac{z-z^{*}}{2}$$

### P1.3
Using Euler's formula, \(e^{j \theta}=\cos \theta+j \sin \theta\) derive the following relations:
1.  $$\cos \theta=\frac{e^{j \theta}+e^{-j \theta}}{2}$$
2.  $$\sin \theta=\frac{e^{j \theta}-e^{-j \theta}}{2 j}$$

### P1.4
1.  Let \(z=r e^{j \theta}\). Express in polar form (i.e., determine the magnitude and angle for) the following functions of \(z\):
    1.  \(z^{*}\)
    2.  \(z^{2}\)
    3.  \(j z\)
    4.  \(z z^{*}\)
    5.  \(\frac{z}{z^{*}}\)
    6.  \(\frac{1}{z}\)
2.  Plot in the complex plane the vectors corresponding to your answers to Problem P1.4a(i)-(vi) for \(r=\frac{2}{3}\), \(\theta=\pi / 6\)

### P1.5
Show that
$$\left(1-e^{j \alpha}\right)=2 \sin \left(\frac{\alpha}{2}\right) e^{j|(\alpha-\pi) / 2|}$$

### P1.6
For \(x(t)\) indicated in Figure P1.6, sketch the following:
1.  \(x(-t)\)
2.  \(x(t+2)\)
3.  \(x(2 t+2)\)
4.  \(x(1-3 t)\)

### P1.7
Evaluate the following definite integrals:
1.  $$\int_{0}^{a} e^{-2 t} d t$$
2.  $$\int_{2}^{\infty} e^{-3 t} d t$$
